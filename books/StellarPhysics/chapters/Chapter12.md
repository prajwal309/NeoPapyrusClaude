# Chapter 12 — Post-Main Sequence Evolution

---

## 12.1 Leaving the Main Sequence

<div data-level="101">

When hydrogen runs out in the core, the star begins to change.

It expands and becomes a **red giant**.

</div>

---

<div data-level="201">

Core hydrogen depletion leads to:

- Core contraction  
- Envelope expansion  
- Increased luminosity  

</div>

---

## 12.2 Red Giant Phase

<div data-level="201">

- Hydrogen shell burning  
- Large radius increase  
- Cooler surface temperature  

</div>

---

<div data-level="301">

Stars move up the **red giant branch (RGB)** in the HR diagram.

Core becomes dense and hot.

</div>

---

## 12.3 Helium Burning

<div data-level="201">

Helium fusion begins:

\[
3\alpha \rightarrow {}^{12}\mathrm{C}
\]

</div>

---

<div data-level="301">

In low-mass stars:

- Helium ignition occurs in a **helium flash** (degenerate core)

In massive stars:

- Smooth ignition  

</div>

---

## 12.4 Advanced Burning Stages

<div data-level="301">

In massive stars:

- Carbon burning  
- Neon burning  
- Oxygen burning  
- Silicon burning  

Leading to iron core formation.

</div>

---

## 12.5 Mass Loss

<div data-level="201">

Stars lose mass through:

- Stellar winds  
- Pulsations  

</div>

---

<div data-level="301">

Mass loss shapes:

- Final stellar mass  
- Evolution pathways  
- Circumstellar environments  

</div>

---

## Summary

- Stars evolve after core hydrogen depletion  
- Red giants form  
- Helium and heavier elements are fused  
- Mass loss becomes significant
