# Chapter 13 — Stellar Deaths

---

## 13.1 The End of a Star

<div data-level="101">

All stars eventually run out of fuel.

What happens next depends on their mass.

</div>

---

## 13.2 White Dwarfs

<div data-level="201">

- Supported by electron degeneracy pressure  
- No fusion  
- Slowly cool over time  

</div>

---

<div data-level="301">

Mass limit:

- Chandrasekhar limit (~1.4 \(M_\odot\))  

</div>

---

## 13.3 Neutron Stars

<div data-level="201">

- Extremely dense  
- Supported by neutron degeneracy pressure  

</div>

---

<div data-level="301">

Formed in supernova explosions.

Contain nuclear-density matter.

</div>

---

## 13.4 Black Holes

<div data-level="201">

- Gravity overwhelms all forces  
- Escape velocity exceeds speed of light  

</div>

---

<div data-level="301">

Characterized by:

- Event horizon  
- Mass, spin  

</div>

---

## 13.5 Supernova Mechanisms

<div data-level="201">

- Core-collapse supernovae  
- Thermonuclear supernovae (Type Ia)  

</div>

---

<div data-level="301">

Supernovae:

- Enrich the interstellar medium  
- Drive galactic evolution  

</div>

---

## Summary

- Stellar death depends on mass  
- Compact objects form  
- Supernovae shape the universe
