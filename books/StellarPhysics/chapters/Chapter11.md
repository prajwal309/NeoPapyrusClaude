# Chapter 11 — Main Sequence Evolution

---

## 11.1 The Longest Phase of a Star’s Life

<div data-level="101">

Most stars spend the majority of their lives quietly burning hydrogen.

This phase is called the **main sequence**.

It is a period of stability — where gravity and fusion are in balance.

</div>

---

<div data-level="201">

During the main sequence:

- Hydrogen is fused into helium in the core  
- Luminosity and temperature evolve slowly  
- Structure remains relatively stable  

The duration of this phase depends strongly on stellar mass.

</div>

---

<div data-level="301">

Main sequence evolution is governed by:

- Core hydrogen depletion  
- Changes in mean molecular weight  
- Structural adjustments (core contraction, envelope expansion)

The star slowly becomes more luminous and larger over time.

</div>

---

## 11.2 Hydrogen Burning Lifetime

<div data-level="201">

Approximate lifetime:

\[
t_{\mathrm{MS}} \sim \frac{M}{L}
\]

Using \(L \propto M^{3.5}\):

\[
t_{\mathrm{MS}} \propto M^{-2.5}
\]

</div>

---

<div data-level="301">

Implications:

- Massive stars → short lifetimes (~Myr)  
- Low-mass stars → long lifetimes (>Gyr to trillions of years)  

</div>

---

## 11.3 Mass–Luminosity Relation

<div data-level="201">

\[
L \propto M^\alpha, \quad \alpha \sim 3-4
\]

This relation governs stellar brightness and evolution.

</div>

---

<div data-level="301">

The relation arises from:

- Hydrostatic equilibrium  
- Energy generation sensitivity to temperature  

It varies across mass ranges and compositions.

</div>

---

## 11.4 Internal Structure Differences

<div data-level="201">

- Low-mass stars → convective envelopes, radiative cores  
- High-mass stars → convective cores, radiative envelopes  

</div>

---

<div data-level="301">

These differences arise from:

- Opacity  
- Energy generation mechanisms (pp vs CNO)  

They influence mixing, evolution, and lifetimes.

</div>

---

## Summary

- Main sequence is the longest phase  
- Lifetime depends strongly on mass  
- Structure varies with mass  
- Stars evolve gradually during this phase
